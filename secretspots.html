Where are all the LSE Secret Study Spots hiding on campus?

Do you ever feel frustrated when you can't find a peaceful study space in the library?
Don't worry we have got you covered! Below is a list of secret study spots that we have put together for you.

  • Clement House Central Stairwell Alcoves, Floors 2-7

    • The building has multiple unique, creative and innovative areas to study, with plugs and desks best suited for a work environment.
    These are located in the middle on most floors (2nd-7th floor) of the building, and each floor accommodates around 4-5 people.

  • Kingsway Ground Floor

    • The entrance of KSW has zoned bench and lounge seating, plants, bright colours, power and a window view of Peacock Theatre.
    This small space has been successfully transformed into an informal place for group studying and simply some catching up with friends. It accommodates around 8-10 students at once.

  • Fawcett House Floor 4

    • This is a truly hidden gem where comfortable, high backed couches with homely cushions are supported by laptop desks and power.
    You can even take a nap on the high backed couches so you don't have to fight for beanbags in the library!

  • Old Building Floors G-3

    • Find recharge spaces with warm lighting, power and wifi in the hidden spots in Old Building.
    These are located at the rear of the floors and each accommodates around 3-4 people.

  • Old Building Basement OLD.B.25 (The Hive Studio)

    • After recent transformation, this room is a collaborative techonology room built on the principles of co-working spaces.
    With group work style high benches, writable surfaces and screen sharing capability, this is a loud computer room which encourages LSE students to study in groups.

  • Peacock Theatre Dress Circle Bar

    • Never knew you would be listening to a lecture in a theatre, let alone study privately in a theatre?
    The theatre's dress circle bar has comfortable seating and study alcoves, power and wifi.
